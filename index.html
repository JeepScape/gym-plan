Protein on Ozempic · Simple Playbook
Target: ~1.6–2.0 g/kg = 140–175 g/day at 88 kg. If appetite is low, aim for the lower end and build up.
  • Use 2 protein shakes/day (25–35 g each). Whey isolate or clear whey are light on the stomach.
  • Make meals protein-first: eggs, Greek yoghurt, cottage cheese, tuna/chicken, lean beef, tofu.
  • Small, frequent portions (5–6) beat big meals when appetite is down.
  • Keep ready-to-eat options: skyr/yoghurt cups, cheese sticks, jerky, tinned fish, rotisserie chicken.
  • Hydrate—sip water between bites; dehydration worsens nausea.
  • If nausea hits, choose lighter textures: soups, smoothies, eggs, yoghurt bowls, clear whey.
Quick day example (~150 g): Shake (30) · Eggs + yoghurt (35) · Chicken wrap (40) · Tuna & crackers (25) · Milk before bed (20).
Week 5 · Increase the Weights
  • If you hit the top of the rep range on every set with good form in Weeks 3–4, add weight in Week 5.
  • Add 2–5 kg on barbell compounds; 1–2 kg on dumbbells/machines; keep 1–2 reps in reserve.
  • Failing early? Reduce the load by ~5–10% and rebuild.
  • Carry on with the same weekly pattern (Sunday start).
Warm‑up 5–8 min easy cardio + 1–2 lighter sets for first two lifts.
Cool‑down 3–5 min walk + light stretch. Aim 7–9k steps/day (training), 9–11k (walk/rest).
If you have persistent nausea or GI issues on Ozempic, speak to your clinician.
